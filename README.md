# 📊 Ad-Hoc Business Insights – SQL + Power BI Project

---

## 📌 Project Overview

This project focuses on solving real-world business problems for **AtliQ Hardware**, a fictional computer hardware company.

The goal was to analyze business data using SQL and present insights through **Power BI visualizations** for top-level management.

The analysis answers **10 ad-hoc business questions**, enabling better decision-making across sales, products, customers, and channels.

---

## 🩷 Business Problem

AtliQ Hardware operates across multiple markets and product segments but lacks quick, data-driven insights.

The management faces challenges such as:

- Difficulty analyzing sales performance across time periods
- Limited visibility into product and segment trends
- Lack of clarity on customer discount strategies
- Heavy dependence on certain sales channels
- No structured way to derive insights from raw data

---

## 💡 Solution

To address these challenges:

- SQL was used to solve **10 business questions**
- Power BI was used to create **clear and interactive visualizations**
- Insights were translated into **business recommendations**

---

## 📊 Key Business Questions Solved

The project answers the following types of questions:

- Markets where the company operates (APAC region)
- Growth in unique products (YoY analysis)
- Product distribution across segments
- Segment-wise product growth
- Products with highest and lowest manufacturing cost
- Customers receiving highest discounts
- Monthly gross sales trends
- Quarterly sales performance
- Channel-wise contribution to revenue
- Top-performing products by division

---

## 🔍 Key Insights

- Unique products increased by ~36%, indicating business expansion
- 83% of products are concentrated in top 3 segments (notebooks, accessories, peripherals)
- Significant gap in manufacturing cost across products
- Top customers receive consistent ~30% discounts
- Sales dropped in early 2020 but strongly recovered in 2021
- Clear seasonality: Q1 highest, Q3 lowest
- Retail channel contributes ~73% of total sales → high dependency
- Top-performing products belong to similar product lines/variants

- Leverage high-performing months for targeted campaigns
- Improve low-performing periods using promotions and bundled offers
